Concept

Turn raw Threads metrics into clear, actionable insights — see what drives engagement, then draft and publish your next post in one place.

The Story

I built ThreadLens after realizing that social media analytics often show what happened — views, likes, and replies — but rarely explain why certain content performs better than others. As more posts accumulated, identifying patterns, understanding audience response, and generating new ideas became increasingly difficult. I wanted to create a workflow that connects reflection and creation in one place, structured as Sync → Analytics → AI Insight → Compose, so creators can learn from past content before writing the next one.

I developed the platform using Next.js and Supabase, integrating the Threads Graph API for content synchronization and AI-assisted insight generation. The system analyzes historical posts to identify recurring patterns, highlight high-performing content, and assist users in drafting new posts while still keeping the creator fully in control of the final result. During development, I also migrated the backend architecture from PocketBase to Supabase to improve scalability, security, and data structure management.

One of the biggest challenges was navigating Meta's App Review process to enable publishing access for users outside the testing environment. Through this project, I learned that building products is not only about writing code, but also about understanding real user workflows, platform limitations, and designing experiences that genuinely help people think and create more effectively.
